Carrier Board Signals - Type 7

New Features of COM Express R3.1 Type 7

  • add support for PCIe Gen4
  • add second PCIe Clock
  • add IPMB interface for Platform Management
  • update 10G Ethernet Interface to support CEI side-band signaling

Connector Location and Pinout Compatibility

Connector positions and pinouts comply with the pinout and signal descriptions within PICMG® COM.0 - COM Express Module Base specification, Revision 3.1. This document includes description of pinouts, signal descriptions and mechanical characteristics of the COM Express specification. The Express-BASE7 R3.1 is compatible with COM Express modules in Basic and Compact form factor, Type 7 pinout, COM.0 Rev. 3.1.

Carrier Board Design

Express-BASE7 R3.1 follows the PICMG COM Express Carrier Design Guide where possible, and its design and schematic have been fully verified. It is recommended to utilize them as references for your carrier board design.

PCIe (PCI Express) Slots

One PCIex16, two PCIex4 and one PCIex8

PCIe 16-31: x16, up to Gen4, support up to 2-slot width devices

PCIe 0-3: x4, up to Gen4

PCIe 4-7: x4, up to Gen3

PCIe 8-15: x8, up to Gen3

JP5 (PCIE MUX): a jumper for PCIe 4-7 slot setting

Jumper Status
1-2 PCIE CONN
2-3 Run BMC (Default, route PCIe 4 lane to BMC module)

10G Ethernet

Up to 4x 10G, dependent on the 10G Ethernet adapter card and COM Express module’s offering. Get details about the 10G Ethernet card concept in 10G Ethernet Card Introduction

10G-KR: up to 4x 10G-KR lanes and its side-band signals. Convert these signals to 10G-SPF+ or 10GBASE-T via different kinds of dedicated 10G Ethernet adapter card if needed.

2.5G Ethernet & mgt. Ethernet

LAN: RJ-45 connector at Rear I/O, up to 2.5GbE speed

mgt.LAN: RJ-45 connector at Rear I/O, up to GbE speed, dedicated for remote management usage and sourced from BMC module

Power Connectors & Power Jumpers

ATX 24 and 4-pin connector offered, together with several jumpers for different power mode setting. the power mode setting also depends on COM Express module

ATX_24: connect the ATX 24-pin (or 20-pin) connector to supply power to the Express-Base7 R3.1 carrier.

ATX_4: connect the ATX 12V 4-pin connector to supply power to the COM Express module.

JP1 (ATX/AT Mode): In AT mode, JP1 shorts PS_ON# to ground directly, forcing power on

Jumper Status
1-2 ATX mode (Default)
2-3 AT mode

JP2 (PWR_OK_Config): Connect the ATX 12V 4-pin connector to supply power to the COM Express module

Jumper Status
1-2 Add 3.3V Pull-up with 10k to signal PWR_OK
3-4 Connect PWRGOOD of ATX power supply (Default)
5-6 Connect PWRGOOD of onboard DC-DC regulator

JP3 (5VSB to Module Source): When an AT power supply is connected to the Express-BASE7 R3.1, JP3 can be used to provide 5VSB to the COM Express module from the carrier board.

Jumper Status
1-2 5VSB from ATX power supply (Default)
2-3 5VSB produced on carrier from Vcc
3-4 No 5VSB to module

JP4 (12V to Module in S3/S5 mode):

  • JP1-2: no 12V to module in S3/S5 mode
  • JP2-3: there’s always 12V input to the module regardless of the module state.
Jumper Status
1-2 12V off in S3/S5
2-3 12V on in S3/S4 (Default)
