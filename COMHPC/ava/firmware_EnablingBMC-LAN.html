Ensuring your BMC firmware has an active LAN

Why?

The BMC LAN is required for, and not limited to, out-of-band monitoring and troubleshooting, and IPMI remote management, which includes functions such as updating the BMC and module’s MMC firmware.


How to check if your BMC has an active LAN or not?

1. Using a self-prepared Ethernet cable, connect a Windows PC as the host machine to your AVA Developer Platform’s BMC GbE port.

2. Set your host machine to a static IP address of 192.168.0.x but different from 192.168.0.100 (the default IP address of AVA Developer Platform’s BMC GbE port), by doing the following:

Expand

A. On your host machine, click the Start menu, go to Settings > Network and Internet > Network and Sharing Center, and click change adapter settings.

B. Right-click the network adapter and select Properties.

C. Select Internet Protocol Version 4 (TCP/IPv4) and click Properties.

D. Select Use the following IP address and define a desired IP address for the host PC (example: 192.168.0.22).

E. Click OK to finish.

​

3. From the host PC, ping your AVA Developer Platform to check if there’s an active LAN connection between them, by doing the following:

Expand

A. On your host machine, click the Start menu, type ‘cmd’, and click to run Command Prompt.

B. In the Command Prompt, type ‘ping 192.168.0.100’ and press Enter to ping your AVA Developer Platform’s BMC GbE port.A.


SUCCESS: Stop here if your pinging of the AVA Developer Platform’s BMC GbE port comes back successful.

FAILURE: Proceed further below if you cannot ping your AVA Developer Platform’s BMC GbE port.

How to activate your BMC LAN?

4. Fully turn off your AVA Developer Platform, with all LEDs off.

5. Remove its glass casing by unscrewing the 2 screws at the right side of its rear panel.

6. Locate the SPI flash sockets labeled EEPROM_0 and EERPOM_1 on the carrier board.

7. Gently pry SPI flash sockets open to reveal the SPI flash chips within.

8. Noting the chip orientation, replace the old SPI flash chips with the new ones you’ve received via postal mail.

9. Close the SPI flash sockets and reattach the system’s glass casing.

10. Turn your AVA Developer Platform back on to finish.
